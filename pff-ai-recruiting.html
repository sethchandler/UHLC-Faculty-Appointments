Rising Stars in Fellowships & VAPs

This is our primary target pool. We should look for the most promising scholars in the VAP/Fellowship market whose work aligns with a pro-innovation perspective, regardless of the prestige of the host institution.

The J.D./Ph.D. Pipeline

Targeting new Ph.D.s who also hold a J.D. from strong, tech-oriented universities is a high-upside strategy. A tenure-track offer at UHLC is a very attractive career path for these candidates.

Who to Look For

Recent Ph.D. graduates in fields like Information Science, Economics, or Public Policy who also have a J.D. and a dissertation focused on AI's market or societal impact.

Where to Look

  • Strong Public Universities: Ph.D. programs at UT Austin, Georgia Tech, University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, Purdue, and Texas A&M.
  • Dissertation Databases: Search ProQuest for recent dissertations combining AI topics with legal or economic analysis.

Lateral Hires from Peer Institutions

Identifying promising, untenured scholars at peer or near-peer schools who may be looking for a more dynamic research environment or the benefits of a major, diverse city like Houston.

Who to Look For

Untenured professors with 2-4 years of teaching experience whose work is gaining traction and who might be attracted to our IPIL program and the university's broader investment in AI.

Where to Look

  • Peer Institutions: Law schools at universities like Florida, Iowa, SMU, and Arizona State.
  • Conference Presenters: Note junior scholars presenting interesting work at major tech law conferences.

The Houston-Centric "Scholar-Practitioner"

Leveraging our location by finding practitioners in the Texas tech and energy sectors who have a demonstrable scholarly interest and are ready to transition to academia. This is our unique home-field advantage.

Who to Look For

Senior associates or junior partners at major Houston or Austin law firms in tech transactions or IP groups, or in-house counsel at tech companies (e.g., Dell, Oracle, HP) who have published articles or taught as adjuncts.

Where to Look

  • Local Bar Associations: The IP sections of the Houston and Austin Bar Associations.
  • Our Adjunct Pool: Review our own adjuncts who teach tech-related courses for potential full-time interest.
